LinkRinse
Privacy policy

Your links are none of our business.

Effective July 12, 2026

The short version: LinkRinse collects nothing, transmits nothing, and has no servers. Every link you rinse is processed entirely on your device and never leaves it.

What LinkRinse does

LinkRinse removes tracking parameters (such as utm_source, fbclid, and gclid) from web links. When you tap Rinse Clipboard — or when the app opens and detects new clipboard content — it reads the link, cleans it on your device, and puts the cleaned version back on your clipboard. iOS shows its standard paste notice whenever the app reads your clipboard, so access is always visible to you and always under your control.

What we collect

Nothing. LinkRinse has:

  • No analytics and no tracking SDKs — this would be a strange app to put them in
  • No advertising
  • No accounts, sign-ins, or identifiers
  • No servers — the app makes no network requests at all

We cannot see your links, your clipboard, your rinse history, or anything else. Not "we don't look" — we can't.

What stays on your device

Your rinse history (the last 50 cleaned links) and your settings (theme, rinse mode) are stored in the app's local storage on your device only. Deleting the app deletes them. They are never uploaded, synced, or shared.

Tips

LinkRinse offers optional tips ("Buy me a coffee") as in-app purchases. Payments are processed entirely by Apple through your Apple account. We never see your payment details — Apple only tells us that a tip happened, with no identity attached.

Third parties

There are none. No third-party code runs in LinkRinse.

Changes to this policy

If LinkRinse ever changes in a way that affects your privacy, we will update this page and the effective date above before the change ships.
