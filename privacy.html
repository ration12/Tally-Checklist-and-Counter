Tally

Privacy Policy

Last updated · July 7, 2026

Tally is a productivity app that helps you manage checklists and count things. This is how we handle your privacy — in plain English.

Tally does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data.

Section 1

What we collect

Nothing. Your checklists, items, counter history, and settings all live on your device. We have no servers, no accounts, no logins. If it's in Tally, it's on your iPhone — and only there.

Section 2

In-app purchases

When you buy Tally Pro, the transaction is handled entirely by Apple. Apple sends us a receipt confirming your purchase — we never see your Apple ID, payment method, name, or address.

Purchase state (whether you own Pro or not) is managed by Apple's StoreKit framework on your device. Restoring purchases queries Apple directly, not us.

Section 3

Advertisements

The free tier of Tally may display a banner advertisement at the bottom of the home screen. Buying Tally Pro removes it.

If Tally integrates a third-party advertising SDK (such as Google AdMob), this policy will be updated to disclose what that SDK collects, and Tally will present Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt before serving personalized ads.

Section 4

Analytics

None. We do not track how you use the app, which features you tap, how often you open it, or anything else. No Firebase, no Mixpanel, no custom telemetry.

Section 5

Children

Tally is rated 4+ and safe for all ages. Because we do not collect any personal data from anyone, we do not knowingly collect data from children.

Section 6

Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, the "Last updated" date at the top of this page will change. Material changes — like adding an advertising SDK — will be called out clearly.
